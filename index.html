Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the Yamuna river in Agra. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

Annual Visitors: 7-8 million
Built: 1632-1653
Architectural Style: Mughal

Special Features:

  • Considered the finest example of Mughal architecture
  • Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site (1983)
  • One of the New Seven Wonders of the World
  • The marble changes color depending on sunlight or moonlight
  • Symmetrical design with intricate pietra dura inlays

Goa Beaches

Goa is famous for its pristine beaches stretching along the Arabian Sea coastline. The former Portuguese colony offers a unique blend of Indian and Western cultures, vibrant nightlife, and water sports activities.

Annual Visitors: 8+ million (including domestic)
Best Time to Visit: November-February
Famous For: Beach shacks, seafood, parties

Special Features:

  • Over 100 km of coastline with diverse beaches
  • Popular beaches include Calangute, Baga, Anjuna, Palolem
  • Portuguese-influenced architecture in Old Goa
  • International yoga retreats and wellness centers
  • Annual Sunburn Festival (Asia's largest EDM festival)

Golden Temple, Amritsar

The Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib) is the holiest Gurdwara and most important pilgrimage site of Sikhism. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, it's known for its stunning golden architecture and the sacred Amrit Sarovar (pool of nectar) surrounding it.

Annual Visitors: 100,000+ daily (30-40 million annually)
Built: 1581-1604 (current structure dates to 1764)
Architectural Style: Sikh with Mughal influences

Special Features:

  • Gilded with 750 kg of pure gold (upper floors)
  • Four entrances symbolizing openness to all people
  • Largest free kitchen (langar) serving 100,000+ daily
  • Continuous recitation of Guru Granth Sahib (holy scripture)
  • Nighttime illumination creates breathtaking reflections

Jaipur, Rajasthan

Known as the "Pink City," Jaipur is the capital of Rajasthan and part of India's Golden Triangle tourist circuit. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, it's renowned for its stunning palaces, forts, and vibrant culture.

Annual Visitors: 15+ million
Founded: 1727
Architectural Style: Rajput with Mughal influences

Special Features:

  • First planned city of India with grid-based design
  • Amber Fort's Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) with intricate mirror work
  • Hawa Mahal's 953 windows allow royal ladies to observe street life
  • Jantar Mantar - largest stone astronomical observatory
  • UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Jantar Mantar and Amber Fort
